## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary**: School administrators, teachers, corporate safety officers, and employees
- **Context**: Desktop-first for threat management, reports, and admin tasks; phone for training content. Used during normal business hours — daytime, not early morning or late night
- **Job to be done**: Manage community safety programs, assess threats, track training, run incident reports. The interface should feel like a competent tool that doesn't add stress to already-serious work

### Brand Personality
**Safe, happy, resilient** — ThreatZero deals with serious subject matter (threats, incidents, safety) but the brand intentionally leans toward empowerment and community strength, not fear or urgency. The tagline is "Empower Communities. Mitigate Threats. Save Lives."

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Warm professionalism. Approachable enough for teachers, credible enough for security officers. Think: a well-run community center, not a war room
- **Anti-references**: Not cluttered, not outdated, not ugly, not D2C, not cold/military/tactical, not urgent/stress-inducing
- **Theme**: Light mode primary. Dark mode is a future consideration but not urgent
- **Color**: Orange (#f7941d) is non-negotiable brand color. Blue secondary works well for trust/action. Open to palette evolution around these anchors
- **Typography**: Currently using system font stack. Open to custom typefaces that reinforce warmth and professionalism

### Design Principles
1. **Calm authority** — The interface handles serious topics with steady confidence. No alarm-red dashboards, no urgency theater. Information is clear and actionable without being stressful
2. **Warm, not soft** — Orange brand warmth carries through the UI. Friendly doesn't mean childish; professional doesn't mean cold. The sweet spot is a competent tool that feels human
3. **Clarity over density** — These users range from tech-comfortable admins to teachers who just need to complete training. Hierarchy, whitespace, and progressive disclosure over cramming everything on screen
4. **Resilient simplicity** — The system supports deep nesting, complex permissions, and multi-org hierarchies, but the UI should make the common case (1-2 levels, straightforward workflows) feel effortless. Complexity is available, not imposed
